# AI Course — Chapter 2 Cheat Sheet

## The Big Question: Why Do We Still Need Human Developers?

> **THE centerpiece of the course. Memorize the 7 pillars; the 90-second answer is the offer-getter.**

## The 5 framings of the question

1. **Hostile** — "isn't your job going away?"
2. **Curious** — "what do you actually do all day?"
3. **Technical** — "what specifically can't AI do?"
4. **Business** — "why pay a senior engineer if AI is 10x cheaper?"
5. **Existential** — "do you worry?"

Each framing wants a different opener. Listen for which one is being asked.

## The 7 Pillars — memorize by name

1. **Judgment** — AI proposes, humans decide.
2. **Context** — only you know your codebase, customers, constraints.
3. **Taste** — AI averages; humans pick the right answer.
4. **Accountability** — AI doesn't sign postmortems.
5. **Communication** — explaining, negotiating, mentoring is human work.
6. **Synthesis** — pulling 5 partial views into one coherent design.
7. **Production discipline** — observability, on-call, debugging at 3am.

## The 90-second master answer

> *"AI is genuinely good at some things and genuinely bad at others. It's good at producing code that exists somewhere in its training data, listing trade-offs, drafting boilerplate. It's bad at judgment, at understanding context the prompt doesn't include, at accountability when something fails. Senior engineering — at least at the level you're hiring for — is mostly the second list. Seven things in particular: judgment, context, taste, accountability, communication, synthesis, production discipline. AI helps me in all seven; it doesn't own any of them. My job, structurally, is to make those decisions well and live with them. That's the work AI hasn't taken, and probably won't take, because someone has to be accountable when reality bites. The reality-biting part has not gone away. If anything, with AI shipping more code faster, it's more important."*

## Common bad answers (avoid)

- *"AI is just autocomplete."* — dismissive, naive.
- *"AI can't really code."* — false, sounds defensive.
- *"AI will be limited for the next 10 years."* — speculative, dodges the question.
- *"I just review what AI writes."* — undersells you.

## Senior soundbites

> *"AI raised the floor. It hasn't lowered the ceiling."*

> *"The accountability isn't negotiable. That's why I'm being paid for this work."*

## If asked in an interview

This is THE question. The 90-second master answer above. **Don't rush; don't ramble. Land it.**
