# AI Course — Chapter 1 Cheat Sheet

## Foundations: The Modern AI-Assisted Engineer

> **"How do you use AI?" is a status check, not a tools question. Answer with structure.**

## The 5 generations of AI tooling

1. **Gen 1 — Stack Overflow age** (pre-2021): manual lookup, manual paste.
2. **Gen 2 — Tab autocomplete** (Copilot, 2021-22): word/line completion.
3. **Gen 3 — Chat-in-editor** (2023): paste-driven, no file context.
4. **Gen 4 — Project-aware chat** (2024): repo context, multi-file edits.
5. **Gen 5 — Agentic editors** (2025+): plan + execute + verify across files. Cascade, Cursor, Aider, Claude Code.

## The 3 workflows

1. **Conversational** — chat back-and-forth, paste code, ask questions.
2. **In-editor inline** — Tab, Cmd-K, multi-line completions.
3. **Agentic** — describe outcome, AI plans + executes across files; you review.

## The 30-second self-intro template

> *"I'm primarily on generation 4-5 — agentic editors for feature work, in-editor inline for refactors, chat for exploration. The mix depends on the task: greenfield agentic, debugging in-editor, design questions in chat. The keyboard time I spend is much less than 2 years ago; the review time is much more. Net throughput is roughly 2-3x, but the texture of the work has changed more than the speed."*

## The "are you a vibe coder?" answer

- **Bad answer**: dismissive — *"no, I'm a real engineer."*
- **Good answer**: *"In the early-prototype phase, yes — I'll let the AI drive. The moment something is going to production or be reviewed, I switch to deliberate, line-by-line ownership."*

## Senior soundbites

> *"AI raised the floor of what's possible. It hasn't lowered the ceiling of what's required."*

> *"I spend less time typing and more time reviewing. The new senior skill is fast, accurate code review."*

## If asked in an interview

> *"How do you use AI in your day-to-day work?"*

Use the 30-second template above. Then **stop talking** — let the interviewer pick a follow-up.
