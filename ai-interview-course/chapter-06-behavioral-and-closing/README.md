# Chapter 6 — Behavioral, Ethical, and Closing

> **The last chapter of the course is the most personal.** Risks, ethics, team dynamics, and the closing narrative that ties your candidacy together. Done well, this is the chapter the interviewer remembers most.

## What you will learn

- The **risks** any senior engineer must articulate about AI: hallucinations, security, intellectual property, accountability.
- How AI changes **team dynamics** — pair programming, code review, ownership, junior mentorship — and the answers interviewers expect.
- Your **portfolio narrative** — a 90-second, memorable story that frames AI use as professional craft rather than crutch — and the closing Q&A that ends the loop strong.

By the end you will be ready to walk into any interview and own the entire AI conversation, from opening to close.

## Lessons

1. **[01-risks-and-accountability.md](./01-risks-and-accountability.md)** — Hallucinations, security risks, IP and licensing, prompt injection, and the chain of accountability when AI ships a bug. Each risk has a concrete answer and a personal commitment.
2. **[02-team-dynamics.md](./02-team-dynamics.md)** — How AI changes pair programming, code review, ownership boundaries, and junior mentorship. The cultural questions interviewers increasingly ask.
3. **[03-portfolio-narrative.md](./03-portfolio-narrative.md)** — Your 90-second AI-assisted-engineer pitch. The 18 prepared questions catalog. The closing questions to ask the interviewer. The 30/60/90-day promise.

## How long it should take

- 30–45 min reading per lesson.
- 60+ min after each lesson rehearsing answers and writing your own portfolio pitch.
- ~5 hours total. Budget extra for the portfolio writing in Lesson 3.

## Why this chapter is the closing chapter

Chapters 1–5 prepared answers to *technical* questions. This chapter prepares answers to *human* questions:

- *"What's a risk you take seriously?"*
- *"How do you mentor a junior on your AI-using team?"*
- *"Tell me your story. Why are you the right hire for this role?"*

Hiring decisions rarely turn on technical questions in the senior loop — most candidates pass those. They turn on whether the interviewers walk out remembering the candidate as someone they trust, want to work with, and can defend to the hiring committee.

This chapter is for that part.

- An interview coming up within the next two weeks. (If not, set yourself a fake deadline. Stakes sharpen the writing.)
